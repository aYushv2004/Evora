How It Works

Evora uses a Mamdani-type fuzzy inference engine to figure out the best charging power for your EV. It looks at three things: how much charge your battery has, what electricity costs right now, and when you need to leave. From that, it recommends a power level between 0 and 22 kW.

The engine runs 20 fuzzy rules on triangular membership functions. Each input gets split into three categories (like Low / Medium / High), and the rules handle everything from "battery is nearly dead and you're leaving in an hour" to "battery is full and electricity is expensive — don't bother." Centroid defuzzification turns the fuzzy result back into a concrete number.
